Best Buy Credit Card Cashback: How to Earn on Extended Warranties

  • Home
  • Blog
  • Blog Details

In today’s fast-paced world, consumers are constantly looking for ways to maximize their spending power. With inflation on the rise and the cost of living increasing, every dollar saved counts. One often-overlooked opportunity to stretch your budget is leveraging cashback rewards from store credit cards—specifically, the Best Buy Credit Card. While many shoppers focus on earning points for purchases, few realize the potential savings tied to extended warranties.

Why Extended Warranties Matter in 2024

The global supply chain crisis and the increasing complexity of electronics have made product reliability a major concern. From smartphones to refrigerators, modern devices are more expensive—and more prone to malfunctions—than ever before. Manufacturers’ warranties often fall short, leaving consumers to foot the bill for repairs or replacements.

This is where Best Buy’s extended warranties shine. Not only do they provide peace of mind, but when paired with the Best Buy Credit Card, they can also become a source of cashback rewards.

How the Best Buy Credit Card Works

The Best Buy Credit Card, issued by Citibank, offers several tiers of rewards:

  • Standard Cardholders: Earn 3% back in rewards on Best Buy purchases.
  • Elite Plus Members: Earn 5% back in rewards (requires spending $3,500 annually at Best Buy).

But here’s the kicker: extended warranties and protection plans also qualify for cashback.

Maximizing Cashback on Extended Warranties

Step 1: Understand What’s Covered

Best Buy’s Geek Squad Protection Plans cover accidental damage, mechanical failures, and even routine wear and tear. Unlike manufacturer warranties, these plans often include:

  • In-home service for large appliances.
  • No lemon policy (if your device needs multiple repairs, you may get a replacement).
  • Battery replacement for laptops and phones.

Since these plans are considered Best Buy purchases, they earn cashback just like any other in-store transaction.

Step 2: Use Your Best Buy Credit Card for Purchases

When buying a new TV, laptop, or appliance, always:

  1. Pay with your Best Buy Credit Card to earn 3-5% back.
  2. Add the extended warranty at checkout—this will also earn rewards.

For example, if you buy a $1,200 laptop with a $200 extended warranty, you’ll earn:

  • $36 cashback (3%) on the laptop.
  • $6 cashback (3%) on the warranty.

Elite Plus members would earn $70 total (5%)—effectively making the warranty cheaper.

Step 3: Stack Rewards with Promotions

Best Buy frequently runs bonus reward promotions, such as:

  • 10% back on first-day purchases for new cardholders.
  • Double rewards during holiday sales.

Timing your warranty purchase with these promotions can double or triple your earnings.

The Hidden Financial Benefits

Beyond cashback, extended warranties can save you thousands in unexpected repair costs. Consider:

  • A $1,500 TV with a $250 warranty may seem expensive—until the screen fails after 18 months, and you get a free replacement.
  • A $2,000 refrigerator with a $300 warranty could save you from a $1,000 compressor repair.

When you factor in cashback rewards, the net cost of the warranty decreases, making it a smarter financial move.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. Not Reading the Fine Print

Some warranties exclude certain types of damage (e.g., water damage on phones). Always verify coverage before purchasing.

2. Forgetting to Activate the Warranty

Some plans require registration within 30 days. Missing this window voids your coverage.

3. Overbuying Coverage

Not every product needs an extended warranty. For cheap electronics, self-insuring (setting aside cash for potential repairs) may be better.

Final Thoughts

In an era where tech reliability is declining and repair costs are soaring, the Best Buy Credit Card offers a rare win-win: cashback on protection plans that shield you from financial headaches. By strategically using your rewards, you can turn warranties into a money-saving tool rather than an added expense.

So next time you’re at Best Buy, don’t just swipe your card—optimize it. Your future self (and wallet) will thank you.
